1 Purpose, Scope and How to Use This Document

This document sets out SDL-HappyClaw's mandatory internal compliance procedures across all Relevant Jurisdictions. It is a living operational manual — not a policy statement — and must be followed whenever the relevant trigger event occurs.

📌 Two Audiences, One Document
Operational use (v1/v2): Follow the step-by-step procedures and checklists. Fill in the blanks. Escalate when required. You do not need to read the regulatory citations to act correctly — the procedures encode them.

Regulator-facing (v4): Each procedure references the specific statutory basis for its requirements. The jurisdiction table in Section 12 maps every obligation to its source. This document, together with completed templates, constitutes SDL-HappyClaw's evidence of compliance.

Scope: This document applies to OneMore Management Ltd (Gibraltar), all employees, contractors and consultants, and (where indicated) referral partners and sub-processors. It covers the following regimes:

  • Data protection: Gibraltar GDPR · UK GDPR (DUAA 2025) · EU GDPR · China PIPL · Nigeria NDPA · Angola Lei 22/11 · Mozambique (constitutional + sectoral)
  • AI governance: EU AI Act · UK ICO AI guidance · China CAC AI measures
  • Financial crime: UK Bribery Act 2010 · ECCTA 2023 · Gibraltar POCA 2015 · FATF
  • Operational resilience: EU DORA · UK FCA SYSC 15A · Gibraltar GFSC OR Guidance

4 Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA / FRIA / PIPIA)

Legal basis: Art. 35 GDPR/UK GDPR/Gibraltar GDPR · EU AI Act Art. 27 (FRIA) · PIPL Arts. 55–56 (PIPIA) · Nigeria NDPA s.28 + GAID Art. 28

⚠️ DPIA Is Mandatory — Do Not Deploy Without One
Every new customer deployment of the Service, and every material change to an existing deployment, requires a DPIA screening. Given the AI/automated processing nature of the Service, the ICO Article 35(4) list, EDPB WP248rev01 nine-criteria test, and GRA guidance all make a full DPIA presumptively mandatory. Do not launch or materially change a deployment without DPO sign-off.
🔍DPIA / FRIA / PIPIA ProcedureOwner: DPO + AI Gov Lead · Before every new deployment
1
Screen for DPIA requirement — Apply EDPB nine-criteria test. If 2+ criteria met, full DPIA required. For any AI deployment touching personal data: DPIA is mandatory (ICO AI Guidance 2024). Before project start
2
Describe the processing — Document the nature, scope, context and purpose; data categories; data flows; sub-processors involved; retention periods.
3
Assess necessity and proportionality — Document the lawful basis; confirm processing is limited to what is necessary; identify and apply data minimisation measures.
4
Identify and assess risks — List risks to data subjects (likelihood × severity). For AI deployments: include bias/discrimination risk, automated decision risk, opacity risk, and output accuracy risk.
5
Identify mitigating measures — Technical controls, contractual controls, procedural controls. Re-assess residual risk after mitigation.
6
FRIA (EU AI Act Art. 27) — For Annex III high-risk use cases: complete the Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment, consulting relevant stakeholders. FRIA may be integrated into the DPIA as a combined document.
7
PIPIA (China PIPL Arts. 55–56) — For PRC deployments involving sensitive PI, ADM, or cross-border transfers: complete PI Protection Impact Assessment. Retain for minimum 3 years.
8
DPO review and sign-off — DPO reviews completed DPIA/FRIA/PIPIA and either: (a) approves; (b) requires additional mitigation before approval; or (c) identifies high residual risk requiring supervisory authority prior consultation. Within 10 Business Days of receipt
9
Prior consultation (if required) — If DPO identifies high residual risk that cannot be mitigated, refer to GRA / ICO / lead supervisory authority before deployment. Allow 8 weeks for SA response
10
Record and monitor — File approved DPIA in compliance system. Set review trigger for material changes. Review at minimum annually for live deployments. Ongoing

DPIA Triggers (Act Immediately)

  • New customer deployment (any Relevant Jurisdiction)
  • New high-risk use case (employment, credit, insurance, public benefit, essential services)
  • New special-category data processing
  • New AI model or material model update in the processing chain
  • New sub-processor with access to personal data
  • New cross-border transfer to a new destination country
  • Material change to automated decision-making logic
  • Security incident that reveals new risk
  • New or amended regulatory guidance identifying high-risk processing type

5 Personal Data Breach — Detection, Response and Notification

🚨 Clock Starts on Detection — Not Confirmation
Under Gibraltar GDPR, UK GDPR, and EU GDPR, the 72-hour notification clock starts when SDL-HappyClaw becomes aware of the breach — i.e., when any employee, contractor or system alert indicates a likely breach — not when the investigation is complete. Start the internal clock immediately.
🚨Data Breach Response SOPOwner: DPO + Technical Lead · 24/7 trigger

Phase 1 — Detect and Contain

1
Detect: Any employee who becomes aware of a potential breach must notify the DPO and Technical Lead immediately by phone/WhatsApp/email. T+0
2
Log: DPO opens a Breach Incident Record in the Breach Register, recording: date/time of detection; nature of suspected breach; estimated data categories affected; estimated number of data subjects; discovery source. T+1 hour
3
Contain: Technical Lead implements immediate containment measures (isolate affected systems; revoke compromised credentials; preserve evidence). Do not delete logs. T+2 hours
4
Notify affected controllers: SDL-HappyClaw (as processor) must notify each affected customer/controller without undue delay. Target: within 4 hours (aligns with DORA Major ICT Incident initial notification for financial-entity clients). T+4 hours

Phase 2 — Assess (Hours 4–48)

5
Risk assessment: DPO assesses: (a) type of breach (confidentiality / integrity / availability); (b) categories and approximate volume of personal data; (c) categories of data subjects; (d) likely consequences; (e) whether special-category or children's data involved.
6
Notification decision: Apply the EDPB Guidelines 9/2022 risk-threshold test. If breach is unlikely to result in risk to data subjects: record in Breach Register; no SA notification required. If likely to result in risk: notify SA within 72 hours. If likely to result in high risk: notify SA within 72 hours AND notify data subjects without undue delay.

Phase 3 — Notify Supervisory Authorities (if required)

Where notification is required, submit to the relevant authority within 72 hours of first awareness:

7
Final report to SA (if notified): Submit final report within 1 month (EU) or as required. Include root cause, full scope, remediation, and preventative measures. T+30 days

All breaches (whether notified or not) must be recorded in the Breach Register. Location: [Internal system path]. Retention: 5 years minimum. Mandatory fields: incident ID; date detected; date notified (SA/subjects); data categories; volume; risk assessment; outcome; lessons learned.

6 Data Subject Rights (DSR) Handling

Legal basis: Arts. 15–22 GDPR · UK GDPR Arts. 15–22 (DUAA amendments from 5 Feb 2026) · PIPL Arts. 44–50 · NDPA s.34 · Lei 22/11 Arts. 11–20

👤DSR Triage and Response ProcedureOwner: DPO · 30-day response clock

Response Timeframes

⏱ GDPR / UK / GI: 1 calendar month (extendable +2 months for complexity) ⏱ China PIPL: 15 working days ⏱ Nigeria NDPA: 14 days (acknowledgement); 30 days (response) ⏱ Angola Lei 22/11: 20 days
1
Receive and log: All DSRs received (by email, web form, post or through a controller) are logged in the DSR Register within 24 hours of receipt. Record: date received; type of request; requester identity; data subjects covered; jurisdiction. Start the response clock. T+24 hours
2
Verify identity: Confirm the requester's identity using reasonable and proportionate means. For UK requests (DUAA s.103): apply the statutory reasonable-search test; "stop the clock" to request clarification where genuinely needed. Do not request excessive documentation. T+3 days
3
Determine scope: SDL-HappyClaw is a processor for most customer data. Redirect most DSRs to the relevant controller (customer) unless SDL-HappyClaw holds its own controller data about the data subject (e.g., the data subject is a contact at a customer or prospect). T+5 days
4
Gather and review data: Search all systems holding relevant data (CRM, logs, backups, sub-processor data). Document the search scope for the regulator file. T+15 days
5
Apply exemptions (if any): Document any applicable exemption (legal professional privilege, crime prevention, third-party rights, etc.). DPO must approve any refusal or partial response. T+20 days
6
Respond in writing: Send response to data subject in clear, plain language. Include: outcome; any data provided; reasons for any refusal; right to complain to supervisory authority. T+30 days (maximum)
7
Close DSR Register entry: Record outcome, date of response, any exemptions applied. On response

ADM-Specific Rights (Art. 22 / Arts. 22A–22D UK)

  • Data subject requests human review of an automated decision → DPO escalates to relevant controller (customer) within 5 Business Days; SDL-HappyClaw provides controller with audit trail of decision inputs
  • Data subject contests ADM outcome → Record request; provide to controller; note in RoPA
  • EU data subject requests meaningful explanation of ADM logic → Provide per Dun & Bradstreet Austria C-203/22 (2025): specific to individual, not generic description

⏳ Mistral AI — Designated Fallback (DPA In Progress — Not Yet Active)
Mistral AI S.A.S. (Paris, France) is SDL-HappyClaw's designated but not yet contracted global fallback LLM provider. Mistral is EU-incorporated, GDPR-native, and not subject to any sanctions or export-control restriction — making it the preferred candidate. Status: Commercial DPA negotiation in progress. Mistral AI must not be used in any customer-facing environment until the DPA is fully executed. Once contracted, activation procedure: (1) Technical Lead detects Anthropic API degradation; (2) Orchestration layer switches to Mistral Large or Mistral Small; (3) DPO notified within 1 hour; (4) Customers notified per SLA resilience clause; (5) Switch back to Anthropic once confirmed. Action owner: Compliance Officer to track DPA completion and report to Board at each quarterly review.
📌 China Mainland Deployment — Alibaba Cloud Qwen (Pre-Approved Candidate)
SDL-HappyClaw does not currently operate a live mainland China AI deployment. Alibaba Cloud (Qwen / 通义千问) is the pre-approved candidate supplier for any future PRC deployment: CAC-filed (regulatory clearance since September 2023); not on the BIS Entity List (verified April 2026); open-source Qwen3 family available under Apache 2.0 licence; enterprise DPA available. Preferred deployment mode: self-hosted Qwen3 open-weights on Alibaba Cloud cn-hangzhou or cn-shanghai — this minimises National Intelligence Law Art. 7 exposure by keeping inference on-premises rather than via Alibaba's hosted API. Secondary option if Alibaba Cloud is unavailable: ByteDance Doubao (CAC-filed; higher reputational risk due to TikTok regulatory scrutiny — requires enhanced due diligence and Board approval before activation). No PRC deployment shall be activated without: (1) current Entity List verification; (2) DPO-approved PIPIA; (3) Alibaba Cloud enterprise DPA executed; (4) Board sign-off. The Compliance Officer reports progress on PRC deployment readiness to the Board quarterly.
