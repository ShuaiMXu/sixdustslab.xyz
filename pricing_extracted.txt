1. What is SDLClaw?
SDLClaw is your own AI employee — one that never sleeps, never takes holidays, and handles dozens of repetitive tasks automatically across your business.
You connect it to the tools you already use — WhatsApp, Telegram, Slack, email, your CRM — and it handles the conversations, tasks, and data work that currently eat your team's time.
Think of it this way:
A small restaurant owner spends 2 hours every day answering the same WhatsApp questions: “What time do you open?” “Do you have tables tonight?” “Is there parking?”
With SDLClaw: those questions are answered instantly, automatically, at 2am if needed, in any language — without the owner touching their phone.
That same agent can take reservations, send reminders, and flag genuinely urgent messages for the owner to handle personally.

2. What Can SDLClaw Do For Your Business?
Below are the eight most common ways businesses use SDLClaw. These are real workflows, not features.

Business benefit with real-world example
01
Answer customer questions — automatically, any hour
Your AI agent handles inbound messages on WhatsApp, Telegram, or your website chat. It reads your FAQ, product catalogue, and policies, then answers instantly.
↳ Real example: A property letting agency receives 40 enquiries a day about available flats. SDLClaw answers all of them, qualifies which are serious, and only forwards hot leads to the agent. Response time drops from 6 hours to 6 seconds.
02
Qualify leads before they reach your team
When someone contacts you, the AI asks the right questions (budget, timeline, what they need), scores the lead, and routes hot prospects to your sales team immediately.
↳ Real example: A solar panel company was spending 3 hours a day on phone calls with people who couldn't afford their product. SDLClaw now pre-qualifies all inbound enquiries. Their sales team only speaks to ready buyers.
03
Book appointments and manage your calendar
The agent checks your availability, offers times, confirms bookings, and sends reminders — without a receptionist or back-and-forth emails.
↳ Real example: A physiotherapy clinic eliminated its receptionist role. Patients book via WhatsApp, receive an automated reminder 24 hours before their appointment, and get a follow-up asking how they're feeling. The clinic saves €1,800/month.
04
Draft and send proposals automatically
Give the agent a client brief and it generates a tailored proposal document, pulls the right pricing, and sends it formatted and ready to sign.
↳ Real example: A freelance web designer used to spend 3 hours on every proposal. SDLClaw generates a first draft in 90 seconds from a short intake form. The designer reviews and sends. Proposal volume tripled.
05
Update your CRM and track every customer interaction
Every conversation, every note, every stage change — the agent logs it automatically so nothing falls through the cracks.
↳ Real example: A recruitment firm had consultants spending 45 minutes a day updating their CRM. SDLClaw logs every call note and candidate update automatically. That's 3.5 hours/week per consultant returned to billable work.
06
Handle Tier 1 support so your team handles Tier 2
The agent resolves password resets, order status queries, returns requests, and basic troubleshooting automatically. Complex issues get escalated with full context.
↳ Real example: An e-commerce store was paying two part-time staff to handle support tickets. SDLClaw now resolves 70% of tickets without human involvement. The two staff were reassigned to customer success and upselling.
07
Send weekly reports to your team or your inbox
Every Monday morning, your agent compiles your key numbers — revenue, pipeline, open tasks, what needs your attention — and delivers a summary wherever you want it.
↳ Real example: A boutique hotel owner now receives a daily 7am briefing via Telegram: last night's revenue, today's check-ins, any pending maintenance requests, and a flag if any review mentions a complaint.
08
Translate, summarise, and process documents
Upload a contract, a supplier invoice, or a research report. The agent reads it, extracts the key points, flags risks, and answers questions about it.
↳ Real example: An import business receives supplier documents in Chinese. SDLClaw translates, extracts pricing and delivery terms, compares them to previous orders, and flags any price increases — in minutes rather than hours.
