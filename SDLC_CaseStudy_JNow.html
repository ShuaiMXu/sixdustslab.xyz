The Challenge

Building a Daily Content Channel with Zero Production Staff

JNow publishes videos about ancient Chinese wisdom — Confucius, Mencius, and Taoism — explained in plain English for curious minds worldwide. New videos land every one to two days. Here is what that actually demands from a solo creator.

1

Content volume

Publishing every 1–2 days requires a relentless production pipeline — research, scripting, recording notes, editing briefs, thumbnails, descriptions, tags, translations.

2

Multilingual reach

The channel covers Chinese philosophy but targets English-speaking audiences globally. Every piece needs cultural translation, not just language translation.

3

SEO & discoverability

YouTube’s algorithm rewards consistency, metadata quality, and engagement signals. A solo creator can’t manually optimise every video’s title, description, tags, chapters, and end screens.

4

Cross-platform distribution

The same content needs adapting for YouTube (long-form), TikTok/Shorts (vertical clips), Instagram (carousels), and Telegram/WhatsApp (community engagement).

5

Research depth

Each video requires accurate philosophical references — primary sources, historical context, proper attribution. Getting this wrong destroys credibility.
