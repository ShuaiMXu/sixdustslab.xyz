1. Introduction

OMM Ltd. (“we”, “us”, “our”), a company incorporated in Gibraltar, operates the SDL-HappyClaw AI employee and agent service. SDL-HappyClaw enables businesses to deploy AI-powered agents across messaging channels to automate customer communications and operational workflows.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect personal data when you interact with our service—whether as a customer, end user, distributor, or website visitor.

By using SDL-HappyClaw, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with our practices, please do not use the service.

3. What Data We Collect

We collect and process the following categories of personal data:

  1. Account & Contact Data — name, email address, telephone number, company name, and billing address.
  2. Service Configuration Data — messaging channel credentials (WhatsApp, Telegram, Slack), automation preferences, and business rules you define within the platform.
  3. Conversation Data — messages processed by the AI agent on behalf of the customer. For this category of data, the customer is the data controller and OMM Ltd. acts as a data processor in accordance with a Data Processing Agreement.
  4. Usage & Analytics Data — service usage statistics, API call volumes, performance metrics, and error logs.
  5. Payment Data — processed by our third-party payment providers (Stripe and/or PayPal). We do not store full payment card details on our systems.
  6. Distributor / Referral Data — distributor name, contact details, referral codes, and commission records.

4. How We Use Your Data

We use the personal data we collect for the following purposes:

  • To provide, maintain, and improve the SDL-HappyClaw service;
  • To process payments and manage subscriptions;
  • To communicate service updates, security alerts, and support information;
  • To calculate and pay distributor commissions;
  • To comply with legal obligations under Gibraltar, EU, and, where applicable, PRC law;
  • To detect, prevent, and address fraud, abuse, and security incidents.

Important commitments:

  • We do not sell personal data to third parties.
  • We do not use customer conversation data to train AI models.

7. International Data Transfers

Our primary data processing takes place in Gibraltar, which is recognised as providing an adequate level of data protection by the European Commission.

Where personal data is transferred outside Gibraltar or the EEA, we rely on the following safeguard mechanisms:

  • EEA transfers: EU Standard Contractual Clauses (Module 2 — Controller to Processor), as referenced in the Service Agreement Annex I Part 2.
  • China (PRC) transfers: China CAC Standard Contract, executed separately and filed with the relevant Chinese authority in accordance with PIPL requirements (Articles 38–39).
  • UK transfers: UK International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA), executed separately when applicable.

We do not transfer personal data to any jurisdiction without an appropriate safeguard mechanism in place.

9. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data, including:

  • Encryption in transit: TLS 1.3 for all data transmitted between clients and our services.
  • Encryption at rest: AES-256 encryption for stored data.
  • Access control: Role-based access control (RBAC) ensuring data is accessible only to authorised personnel.
  • Security audits: Regular security assessments and penetration testing.
  • Incident response: Documented incident response procedures (see Operations Manual, Chapter 4). In the event of a personal data breach, we will notify the relevant supervisory authority and affected individuals in accordance with GDPR Articles 33–34.

10. Your Rights (GDPR)

Under the General Data Protection Regulation, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data:

  • Right of access — obtain confirmation of whether we process your data and request a copy.
  • Right to rectification — request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure (“right to be forgotten”) — request deletion of your data where there is no compelling reason for its continued processing.
  • Right to restriction of processing — request that we limit how we use your data.
  • Right to data portability — receive your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format.
  • Right to object — object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing.
  • Right to withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent, withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.
  • Right to lodge a complaint — with the Gibraltar Regulatory Authority or your local supervisory authority.

11. China-Specific Rights (PIPL)

Where the Personal Information Protection Law of the People’s Republic of China (PIPL) applies to the processing of your personal information, you have the following additional rights:

  • Right to know and decide — be informed about and make decisions regarding the processing of your personal information.
  • Right to restrict or refuse processing — restrict or refuse the processing of your personal information, except where laws or administrative regulations provide otherwise.
  • Right to access, copy, correct, and delete — access, obtain copies of, correct, and request deletion of your personal information.
  • Right to request explanation — request an explanation of the rules governing the processing of your personal information.

Cross-border transfers of personal information originating in the PRC are subject to the protections set out in PIPL Articles 38–39. We execute the China CAC Standard Contract for such transfers and ensure that overseas recipients provide a level of protection not lower than that required under PRC law.
