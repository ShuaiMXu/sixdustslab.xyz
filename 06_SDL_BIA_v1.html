📌 Nature of this Agreement
This Agreement creates a non-exclusive, independent-contractor relationship for the introduction of prospective customers to SDL-HappyClaw. It does not create a partnership, joint venture, employment, agency, franchise or distributorship. The Introducer has no authority, express or implied, to bind SDL-HappyClaw to any contract, to make any representation on SDL-HappyClaw's behalf, or to accept any payment from a prospective customer.

2 Definitions

"Applicable Law"
All laws, regulations, binding guidance, codes of practice and regulatory requirements applicable to either Party in any jurisdiction in which it operates or in which Qualified Introductions are made, including without limitation data protection law, anti-bribery and corruption law, anti-money laundering law, sanctions law, and financial services law.
"Commencement Date"
The date on which both Parties have signed this Agreement as recorded in the Signature Block (Section 15).
"Commission"
The fee payable by SDL-HappyClaw to the Introducer in respect of a Qualified Introduction, calculated in accordance with Section 5.
"Commission Period"
The period during which Commission is payable in respect of a Qualified Introduction, as specified in Section 5.3.
"Confidential Information"
All non-public information disclosed by one Party to the other in connection with this Agreement, including pricing, customer lists, technical specifications, business strategy, and the terms of this Agreement.
"Customer Contract"
A validly executed Master Services Agreement (or equivalent) between SDL-HappyClaw and an Introduced Customer for the provision of the SDL-HappyClaw Service.
"Effective Commission Revenue"
The net revenue actually received by SDL-HappyClaw from an Introduced Customer in respect of the Service, excluding VAT, withholding taxes, refunds, chargebacks, and one-time setup fees unless expressly agreed in an Order Form.
"Introduced Customer"
A prospective customer whose details are submitted to SDL-HappyClaw by the Introducer as part of a Qualified Introduction, who has not previously been in SDL-HappyClaw's customer relationship management system, and who executes a Customer Contract within the Registration Window.
"Introducer"
The party identified as Introducer in Section 1. The Introducer acts as an independent contractor and not as an agent, employee, distributor, partner, franchisee or legal representative of SDL-HappyClaw.
"Lead Registration"
A written or electronic submission by the Introducer to SDL-HappyClaw, using SDL-HappyClaw's prescribed Lead Registration Form, identifying a prospective customer and providing sufficient information to enable SDL-HappyClaw to assess and respond to the introduction.
"Qualified Introduction"
A Lead Registration that: (i) identifies a prospective customer that is not already known to SDL-HappyClaw or registered by another introducer within the preceding 12 months; (ii) is accepted in writing by SDL-HappyClaw within the Acceptance Window; (iii) results in the execution of a Customer Contract within the Registration Window; and (iv) arises from the Introducer's bona fide activity within the Permitted Scope.
"Registration Window"
The period of 12 months from the date on which SDL-HappyClaw accepts a Lead Registration in writing, during which a Qualified Introduction must result in a signed Customer Contract to be eligible for Commission.
"Relevant Jurisdiction"
Has the meaning given in the Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) and includes, without limitation, Gibraltar, the United Kingdom, all EU Member States, the People's Republic of China, Nigeria, Angola, and Mozambique.
"SDL-HappyClaw Brand Assets"
SDL-HappyClaw's trademarks, trade names, logos, service marks, and any other proprietary brand materials made available to the Introducer under Section 6.
"Service"
Has the meaning given in the SDL-HappyClaw Acceptable Use Policy as updated from time to time.

3 Appointment and Scope

3.1 Non-Exclusive Appointment

SDL-HappyClaw appoints the Introducer on a non-exclusive basis to introduce prospective customers for the Service within the Permitted Scope set out in Schedule 1. SDL-HappyClaw retains the right to appoint other introducers, referral partners, distributors and resellers in any territory or market without restriction.

3.2 Permitted Scope

Unless otherwise specified in Schedule 1, the Permitted Scope covers introduction of prospective B2B customers in the Introducer's primary market(s) of operation. The Introducer shall not approach prospective customers outside the Permitted Scope without SDL-HappyClaw's prior written consent.

3.3 No Authority to Bind

The Introducer has no authority to:

  • Enter into any contract or legally binding commitment on SDL-HappyClaw's behalf.
  • Make any representation, warranty or guarantee regarding the Service beyond the content of SDL-HappyClaw's published marketing materials.
  • Negotiate pricing, contract terms or service levels on SDL-HappyClaw's behalf.
  • Accept any payment from a prospective customer on SDL-HappyClaw's behalf.
  • Represent that the Introducer is an agent, employee, partner or legal representative of SDL-HappyClaw.

3.4 Acceptance Window

Upon receipt of a Lead Registration, SDL-HappyClaw will respond in writing within 5 Business Days to confirm acceptance or rejection. If SDL-HappyClaw does not respond within 5 Business Days, the Lead Registration is deemed rejected. SDL-HappyClaw may reject a Lead Registration without giving reasons.

4 Introducer Obligations

4.1 General Conduct

The Introducer shall:

  • Conduct all introduction activities honestly, professionally and in good faith.
  • Represent the Service accurately and only using SDL-HappyClaw's current approved materials.
  • Immediately notify SDL-HappyClaw of any actual or potential conflict of interest.
  • Maintain adequate resources, expertise and authorisations to fulfil its obligations under this Agreement.
  • Keep SDL-HappyClaw informed of material developments in each Relevant Jurisdiction that may affect the Introducer's activities or SDL-HappyClaw's compliance obligations.

4.2 Regulatory Compliance

The Introducer warrants, represents and undertakes on an ongoing basis that:

  • The Introducer's activities under this Agreement do not require SDL-HappyClaw to obtain any regulatory licence, registration or authorisation in any Relevant Jurisdiction.
  • The Introducer holds all licences, authorisations, registrations and permissions required by Applicable Law for the Introducer's own activities under this Agreement.
  • The Introducer will promptly notify SDL-HappyClaw if any such licence or authorisation is revoked, suspended or materially varied.

4.3 Anti-Bribery and Corruption

The Introducer unconditionally undertakes to:

  • Comply with the UK Bribery Act 2010, the Gibraltar Crimes Act 2011 (as amended), the UK Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act 2023 (failure to prevent fraud, in force 1 September 2025), and all equivalent anti-corruption and anti-fraud legislation in any Relevant Jurisdiction.
  • Not offer, promise, give, authorise, request or accept any financial or other advantage, directly or indirectly, in connection with this Agreement or any introduction made under it, in breach of applicable anti-bribery law.
  • Not pay or receive any facilitation payment in connection with this Agreement.
  • Not engage in any act, omission or scheme that constitutes fraud or dishonest misrepresentation under Applicable Law.
  • Maintain adequate procedures to prevent bribery and fraud by persons associated with the Introducer, consistent with the UK Ministry of Justice Guidance on the Bribery Act 2010 (Six Principles) and the Home Office Guidance on failure to prevent fraud.
  • Maintain complete and accurate records of all expenditure, gifts and hospitality relating to this Agreement, available for SDL-HappyClaw's inspection on reasonable notice.
⚠️ Gifts and Hospitality Limits
The Introducer must obtain SDL-HappyClaw's written pre-approval before providing any gift or hospitality to a third party in connection with SDL-HappyClaw business exceeding the following thresholds: £100 / €120 / USD 125 per occasion (notification); £250 / €300 / USD 310 (executive approval); £500 / €600 / USD 620 (prohibited without board resolution). All gifts and hospitality must be recorded in the Introducer's register and disclosed to SDL-HappyClaw quarterly.

4.4 Sanctions and Export Controls

The Introducer warrants on an ongoing basis that:

  • Neither the Introducer, nor its directors, officers, ultimate beneficial owners, nor (to its knowledge) any prospective customer it introduces, is designated on any applicable sanctions list, including the UK Consolidated Sanctions List, the EU Consolidated Financial Sanctions List, the UN Consolidated List, the US OFAC SDN List, or the US BIS Entity List.
  • The Introducer will perform sanctions screening on each prospective customer prior to Lead Registration and promptly notify SDL-HappyClaw of any match.
  • The Introducer will not use the Service, or cause SDL-HappyClaw to provide the Service, in any manner that would violate any applicable sanctions, export control or trade-embargo regime.
  • The Introducer has conducted enhanced due diligence on any prospective customer located in or connected to a jurisdiction subject to comprehensive sanctions or elevated export-control risk.
⚠️ Entity List Notice
The Introducer must not introduce any prospective customer that is listed on any applicable jurisdictional sanctions list where such introduction would cause SDL-HappyClaw or its subprocessors to violate applicable laws or sanctions regulations. The Introducer acknowledges that the SDL-HappyClaw Service uses AI technologies originating from multiple jurisdictions, and that onboarding entities subject to sanctions may require regulatory authorization, which may be subject to a presumption of denial..

4.5 Anti-Money Laundering

The Introducer shall:

  • Conduct customer due diligence (CDD) on each prospective customer prior to Lead Registration, including identification and verification of the legal entity, its directors and ultimate beneficial owners (25% threshold), and the source of its funds where required by Applicable Law.
  • Apply enhanced due diligence (EDD) to prospective customers that are politically exposed persons (PEPs), their family members or close associates, or that are located in high-risk jurisdictions as identified by FATF.
  • File Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) with the Gibraltar Financial Intelligence Unit (GFIU) or the equivalent authority in the Introducer's jurisdiction if the Introducer knows or suspects that a prospective customer is engaged in money laundering or terrorist financing, and inform SDL-HappyClaw (subject to tipping-off restrictions).
  • Retain CDD and EDD records for a minimum of 5 years from the date of the relevant transaction or the termination of the relationship, whichever is later.

4.6 Reporting and Co-operation

The Introducer shall provide SDL-HappyClaw with a written quarterly report (within 15 days of each calendar quarter-end) detailing: (i) lead-generation activity during the quarter; (ii) the pipeline of prospective customers in active discussion; (iii) any material changes to the Introducer's business, ownership or regulatory status; and (iv) a summary of gifts and hospitality incurred. SDL-HappyClaw retains audit rights exercisable on 10 Business Days' notice to verify the Introducer's compliance with this Agreement.

5.2 Commission Calculation and Payment

1
Trigger: Customer Contract executed and first payment received by SDL-HappyClaw from the Introduced Customer.
2
Calculation: SDL-HappyClaw calculates Commission on Effective Commission Revenue actually received in each calendar month, within 15 days of month-end.
3
Statement: SDL-HappyClaw issues a Commission Statement within 20 days of each calendar month-end, showing gross revenue, any deductions, and net Commission payable.
4
Payment: Commission shall be paid in GBP or EUR within thirty (30) days of the date of the relevant Commission Statement, subject to the Introducer having submitted a valid invoice in the required format. Payment shall be made by bank transfer or other electronic funds transfer method (including via regulated payment institutions) to an account designated by the Introducer.
5
Dispute: Introducer must raise any dispute within 30 days of receiving a Commission Statement. Undisputed amounts are binding if not challenged within that period.

5.3 Commission Period

Commission is payable for the period set out in Section 5.1, commencing on the date the Introduced Customer's first payment is received by SDL-HappyClaw, subject to a maximum Commission Period of 36 months unless extended by written agreement.

5.4 Commission Conditions and Clawback

Commission is subject to the following conditions:

  • Commission is only payable while this Agreement is in force and the Introducer is not in material breach of it.
  • If an Introduced Customer's Customer Contract is terminated, Commission ceases from the date of termination.
  • If Commission has been paid in respect of revenue that is subsequently refunded or charged back, SDL-HappyClaw may deduct the over-paid amount from future Commission statements.
  • SDL-HappyClaw may withhold Commission pending resolution of any bona fide dispute regarding the Introducer's compliance with Section 4.
  • Clawback: If SDL-HappyClaw discovers that a Qualified Introduction was procured in breach of Section 4 (including through bribery, fraud, misrepresentation or sanctions violation), all Commission paid in respect of that introduction becomes immediately repayable by the Introducer.

5.5 Taxes and Withholding

Each Party is responsible for its own taxes on Commission received. If SDL-HappyClaw is required by Applicable Law to withhold tax on Commission payments, it will deduct the required amount and provide the Introducer with appropriate withholding tax documentation. The Introducer is responsible for obtaining any tax treaty relief or foreign-tax credits available in its jurisdiction.

5.6 Commission Cap — Anomalous Payments

No individual Commission payment shall exceed 20% of the annualised first-year Effective Commission Revenue of the relevant Introduced Customer without prior written approval from SDL-HappyClaw's board. Payments exceeding this cap without board approval are voidable at SDL-HappyClaw's election.

  • Not share personal data with SDL-HappyClaw that the Introducer is not lawfully authorised to share.

The Introducer shall indemnify SDL-HappyClaw against all losses, damages, costs, expenses (including reasonable legal fees), fines, penalties and regulatory sanctions arising from or in connection with:

  • Any act or omission of the Introducer that causes SDL-HappyClaw to breach Applicable Law;

11 Term and Termination

11.1 Initial Term

This Agreement commences on the Commencement Date and continues for an initial term of 12 months, unless terminated earlier in accordance with this Section.

11.2 Automatic Renewal

Unless either Party gives written notice of non-renewal at least 30 days before the end of the then-current term, this Agreement automatically renews for successive 12-month periods on the same terms, subject to any updates notified by SDL-HappyClaw.

11.3 Termination for Convenience

Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience on 30 days' written notice to the other Party.

11.4 Termination for Cause

Either Party may terminate this Agreement with immediate effect on written notice if the other Party:

  • Commits a material breach of this Agreement that is incapable of remedy;
  • Commits a material breach that is capable of remedy and fails to remedy it within 15 Business Days of receiving written notice specifying the breach;
  • Becomes insolvent, enters administration, liquidation or any analogous process;
  • Is the subject of a sanctions designation, or a director or beneficial owner is designated.

SDL-HappyClaw may additionally terminate immediately, or suspend Commission payments pending investigation, if it has reasonable grounds to suspect a breach of Sections 4.3, 4.4 or 4.5.

11.5 Consequences of Termination

  • Upon termination, the Introducer's brand licence (Section 6.1) is immediately revoked and all Brand Assets must be returned or destroyed.
  • Commission accrued and payable on Introduced Customers' contracts that pre-date termination continues for the remainder of the Commission Period, unless termination is for the Introducer's material breach or sanctions/ABAC breach, in which case Commission is forfeited.
  • Sections 4.3, 4.4, 4.5, 9, 10.4, 11.5, 12 and 13 survive termination.
  • SDL-HappyClaw will delete the Introducer's personal data in accordance with its Privacy Notice, save where retention is required by Applicable Law.

Addendum A — European Union & United Kingdom

Supplemental terms · Effective 1 May 2026

A.1 FCA Regulated Activities — UK

The introduction of prospective customers for a B2B AI SaaS service does not constitute a regulated activity under the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (FSMA) in the ordinary case. However, if the Service is used by an Introduced Customer in connection with regulated financial services, the Introducer must confirm in the Lead Registration Form whether the prospective customer is FCA-regulated and whether the introduction itself would require the Introducer to be authorised as an Appointed Representative or Introducer Appointed Representative. SDL-HappyClaw will not accept Lead Registrations that would require SDL-HappyClaw to become a principal firm without prior board approval.

A.2 UK Bribery Act 2010 — Adequate Procedures Confirmation

UK-based Introducers must confirm at onboarding and annually that they have adopted written anti-bribery procedures proportionate to the risks of their business, consistent with the Ministry of Justice Six Principles Guidance. SDL-HappyClaw will request a copy of the Introducer's ABAC policy as part of its third-party due diligence. Failure to provide adequate ABAC documentation within 20 Business Days of request entitles SDL-HappyClaw to suspend Commission payments.

A.3 Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act 2023 — Failure to Prevent Fraud

In force from 1 September 2025. UK Introducers must not engage in, or cause persons associated with them to engage in, any fraud that benefits SDL-HappyClaw (including misrepresentation of the Service to prospective customers, false Commission claims, or creation of false Lead Registrations). SDL-HappyClaw has adopted a Failure to Prevent Fraud Policy, a copy of which is available on request.

A.4 EU General Data Protection Regulation — Independent Controllers

Where the Introducer shares personal data of EU data subjects with SDL-HappyClaw, the Introducer must ensure it has a lawful basis for such transfer and that the transfer complies with Chapter V GDPR (EU SCCs where required). SDL-HappyClaw provides an EU Controller-to-Controller Standard Contractual Clauses schedule on request.

A.5 DORA (EU Financial-Sector Clients)

Where an Introduced Customer is an EU financial entity subject to DORA (Regulation (EU) 2022/2554), the Introducer must disclose this at Lead Registration stage so that SDL-HappyClaw can ensure that the Customer Contract contains the DORA-compliant contractual provisions required under DORA Article 30. Commission is conditional on the Introduced Customer executing a DORA-compliant annex.
