📌 DORA Enterprise Agreement — Purpose
This Agreement is SDL-HappyClaw's DORA-compliant ICT third-party service agreement for EU financial entities subject to Regulation (EU) 2022/2554 (Digital Operational Resilience Act). It satisfies the mandatory contractual provisions under DORA Articles 30(2) and 30(3) and the Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2024/1505 (RTS on ICT third-party risk). It operates as a standalone agreement OR as an addendum to the SDL-HappyClaw AI Agent Service Agreement v2.0 (base agreement). Where used as an addendum, this agreement prevails in the event of any conflict with the base agreement solely to the extent required for DORA compliance.
🏦 Who Must Use This Agreement
This Agreement is mandatory for any Customer that is: a credit institution; payment institution; electronic money institution; investment firm; insurance or reinsurance undertaking; crypto-asset service provider; or any other entity within the scope of DORA Article 2 that uses the SDL-HappyClaw AI Agent Service in connection with functions that constitute or support critical or important functions. 3.5 Termination Assistance and Data Return (Art. 30(2)(f))

  • Customer may terminate this Agreement with 6 months' written notice (or immediately for material breach)

  • All Customer Data deleted from SDL-HappyClaw systems within 60 days of termination confirmation, with written certification of deletion

4 Critical / Important Function Requirements (DORA Art. 30(3))

📌 Applicability
Section 4 applies where Customer has designated the SDL-HappyClaw AI Agent Service as supporting a critical or important function within its DORA ICT risk management framework. Customer must notify SDL-HappyClaw of this designation in the Pricing Schedule / Order Form.

4.1 Full SLA with Quantitative and Qualitative Performance Targets

For critical/important function deployments, the full SLA set out in Section 3.1 applies with additional monitoring: (a) monthly availability report delivered by the 5th day of the following month; (b) quarterly performance review meeting; (c) quarterly SLA performance attestation signed by SDL-HappyClaw's compliance officer.

4.2 Business Continuity and Contingency Plans (Art. 30(3)(b))

  • SDL-HappyClaw maintains a Business Continuity Plan (BCP) and Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP) covering the SDL-HappyClaw AI Agent Service
  • Customer may request a copy of the BCP/DRP summary (non-sensitive portions) annually
  • SDL-HappyClaw will participate in Customer's BCP/DR testing exercises on reasonable prior notice (up to twice per year; additional participation at prevailing support rates)
  • Fallback AI model (Mistral AI, subject to DPA execution) provides secondary AI inference capacity. Customer will be notified of fallback model status updates.

4.3 Audit and Inspection Rights (Art. 30(3)(c))

  • Annual audit right: Customer (and its competent authority) may conduct one audit per calendar year of SDL-HappyClaw's ICT services relevant to the Customer's critical/important functions, on 30 days' advance written notice
  • Audit scope: Security controls, access logs, incident records, business continuity documentation, sub-processor chain. Excludes commercially sensitive information relating to other customers.
  • Competent authority access: SDL-HappyClaw agrees to provide the Customer's competent supervisory authority (EBA, ESMA, EIOPA, or national authority) full access and information rights as required by DORA
  • Pooled audits: SDL-HappyClaw may satisfy audit requests from multiple customers through a pooled audit arrangement where legally permissible
  • Third-party audit reports: SDL-HappyClaw will share available third-party audit reports (ISO 27001, SOC 2) as a substitute for or supplement to Customer audits

4.4 TLPT Cooperation (Art. 30(3)(d) / Art. 26-27)

Where SDL-HappyClaw is within the scope of Customer's Threat-Led Penetration Test (TLPT) under DORA Article 26, SDL-HappyClaw agrees to: (a) cooperate with the Customer and its lead authority to enable the TLPT; (b) provide necessary technical access and information to the testers; (c) coordinate on timing to minimise service disruption. TLPT activities will be governed by a separate TLPT Cooperation Agreement to be executed at least 60 days before the planned test date.

4.5 Exit Strategy (Art. 30(3)(e))

SDL-HappyClaw maintains a customer exit strategy covering:

  • 6-month minimum transition period for critical/important function customers
  • Data portability: full Customer Data export in JSON/CSV format; API documentation; Skills configuration export
  • Knowledge transfer: up to 40 hours technical assistance during transition (at prevailing rates)
  • Substitutability plan: Customer's competent authority may request evidence that alternative ICT providers could provide equivalent services; SDL-HappyClaw will provide a substitutability assessment within 30 days of request
  • Subcontracting chain exit: SDL-HappyClaw's key sub-processors (Anthropic, AWS, GCP) each maintain their own data portability and exit provisions per their commercial terms

5 ICT Incident Classification and Reporting (DORA Art. 18-20)

5.1 Major ICT Incident Criteria

SDL-HappyClaw applies the DORA RTS criteria (Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2024/1505) to classify incidents as Major:

  • Duration and geographic spread exceeding EBA/ESA thresholds
  • Economic impact (gross direct cost) exceeding €100,000 or 0.1% of total annual net revenue
  • Reputational impact affecting public trust
  • Data loss or integrity breach affecting critical data
  • Critical/important function unavailability exceeding 30 minutes during business hours

5.2 Regulatory Reporting Support

Where Customer is required to submit an initial, intermediate, or final report to its competent authority under DORA Article 19, SDL-HappyClaw will provide all SDL-HappyClaw-side information required for that report within the timelines specified in Section 3.4, and will designate a named DORA incident contact available 24/7 during any active Major ICT Incident.

6 Information Rights and Penetration Testing

6.1 Continuous Information Obligations

  • Monthly: Tier A availability report, incident log summary
  • Quarterly: SLA performance attestation, sub-processor list confirmation, security patch status
  • Annually: Third-party audit/certification status (ISO 27001, SOC 2); BCP/DRP summary; staff training completion; penetration test summary (sanitised)
  • On request: ICT risk register (relevant portions); change management log; access control review results

6.2 Penetration Testing

SDL-HappyClaw conducts annual penetration testing of the SDL-HappyClaw AI Agent Service infrastructure. Results (executive summary, critical and high findings, remediation status) are shared with DORA customers on request, subject to confidentiality obligations.

7 Exit Strategy Documentation

SDL-HappyClaw will provide to Customer, within 30 days of execution of this Agreement and updated annually:

  • Substitutability assessment: description of alternative ICT providers capable of providing equivalent services
  • Transition plan template: step-by-step migration guide for each service component
  • Data export format specification: technical documentation for Customer Data extraction
  • API documentation: sufficient for Customer to operate independently or migrate to an alternative provider
  • Subcontracting chain map: visual representation of SDL-HappyClaw's ICT sub-contractors

10 General Provisions

10.1 Relationship to Base Agreement

This DORA Enterprise Agreement operates as a standalone agreement or as an addendum to the SDL-HappyClaw AI Agent Service Agreement v2.0. Where used as an addendum, in the event of any conflict between this Agreement and the base agreement, this Agreement prevails solely to the minimum extent required to satisfy DORA requirements. All other provisions of the base agreement apply.

10.2 Governing Law and Arbitration

This Agreement is governed by the laws of Gibraltar. Any dispute shall be resolved by arbitration under the UNCITRAL Arbitration Rules, seated in Gibraltar, language English, sole arbitrator (appointed by LCIA failing agreement). Nothing in this clause prevents either party from seeking urgent relief from any court of competent jurisdiction or from a competent authority exercising its DORA supervisory powers.

10.3 Regulatory Cooperation

SDL-HappyClaw agrees to cooperate fully with Customer's competent authority (EBA, ESMA, EIOPA, or national authority) in the exercise of its supervisory powers under DORA, including providing information, access, and assistance as required.

10.4 Register of Information

SDL-HappyClaw will provide Customer with all information required to complete its Register of Information under DORA Article 28(3), including a pre-populated template covering SDL-HappyClaw's ICT services, within 30 days of execution and updated annually.

10.5 Amendment

This Agreement may be amended by mutual written agreement. SDL-HappyClaw will notify Customer of any amendment required to maintain DORA compliance with not less than 30 days' advance notice. If a mandatory regulatory requirement requires immediate amendment, SDL-HappyClaw will notify Customer as soon as practicable.
